10 AMAZING PLACES TO VISIT IN GHANA!

The Kakum National Park

1

Kakum National Park is home to many endangered animals and has a canopy walk. It is located in the Easten Region of Ghana.

Larabanga Mosque

2

The Larabanga Mosque, built in 1421 is the oldest in Ghana and is located in Larabanga in the Savannah Region of Ghana. It is usually refered to as "the Mecca of West Africa".

Boti falls

3

Boti Falls is a twin waterfall that are said to represent a male and a female.It located in the Eastern Region of Ghana, 17km northeast of Koforidua

Aburi Botenical Gardens

4

The Aburi Botanical Gardens in Ghana, established in 1890, cover 64.8 hectares and house diverse tropical plants and exotic trees. A peaceful destination for nature lovers, the gardens offer scenic views and leisurely strolls in a serene environment.

The Lake Volta

5

The worlds largest artifical lake; spans 8,502 sq km. It provides Ghana and neibouring countries with electricity and teems with tourism and biodiversity.

Mole Park

6

Mole National Park in Ghana is the country's largest protected area, celebrated for its rich wildlife like elephants and antelopes. Its vast savannahs and guided safaris offer an authentic African ecotourism experience.

Larbadi Beach

7

Labadi Beach in Accra, Ghana, is a popular coastal destination known for swimming, cultural events, and vibrant atmosphere. It's famous for local cuisine, music, and stunning sunsets.

Paga Crocodile Pond

8

Discover Ghana's Paga Crocodile Pond: a sacred oasis where humans and crocodiles coexist peacefully. Touch history, feel the culture, and witness a unique bond between man and nature. Your unforgettable adventure awaits!

The Elmina Castle

9

Elmina Castle stands as a haunting testament to history on Ghana's coast. Built in 1482 by the Portuguese this iconic fortress witnessed centuries of the transatlantic slave trade. Its walls echo with the past, serving as a powerful reminder of human resilience and the need to remember our shared history.

Okomfo Anokye Sword Site

10

Situated on the grounds of the Komfo Anokye Teaching Hospital, Kumasi, is the Komfo Anokye Sword Site which is seminal in Asante history. An over 300 year old sword wedged in the rockface is one of the centre artefacts and legends of the rich history of the Asante nation.
