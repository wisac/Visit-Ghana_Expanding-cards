10 AMAZING PLACES TO VISIT IN GHANA!

The Kakum National Park

1

Kakum National Park, situated in the Eastern Region of Ghana, is home to numerous endangered animals and features a captivating canopy walk. The exhilaration of strolling 40 meters above the forest floor is an unforgettable experience you won't want to miss!

Larabanga Mosque

2

The Larabanga Mosque, constructed in 1421, stands as the oldest mosque in Ghana and is situated in Larabanga, within the Savannah Region of Ghana. Often referred to as "the Mecca of West Africa.", the mosque houses a very old Koran which is thought to have been delivered from heaven to an Imam called Bramah, around 1650, after he had prayed hard for a Koran.

Paga Crocodile Pond

3

Explore Ghana's Paga Crocodile Pond, a sacred sanctuary where humans and crocodiles peacefully coexist. Immerse yourself in history, experience the local culture, and witness the extraordinary bond between humanity and nature. Your unforgettable adventure awaits!

Boti falls

4

Boti Falls is a twin waterfall said to symbolize a male and a female. The two waterfalls converge in the middle, creating a beautiful and colorful rainbow, which the local people interpret as "a mating ceremony." It is situated in the Eastern Region of Ghana, approximately 17 kilometers northeast of Koforidua. Read more

The Elmina Castle

5

Elmina Castle stands as a haunting testament to history on Ghana's coast. Constructed in 1482 by the Portuguese, this iconic fortress bore witness to centuries of the transatlantic slave trade. Its walls resonate with the echoes of the past, serving as a poignant reminder of human resilience and the importance of acknowledging our shared history. A visit to Elmina Castle is a solemn yet essential experience that fosters profound understanding and reflection.Read more

Aburi Botenical Gardens

6

TThe Aburi Botanical Gardens in Ghana, founded in 1890, encompass 64.8 hectares and are home to a wide variety of tropical plants and exotic trees. This tranquil destination is a haven for nature enthusiasts, providing picturesque vistas and leisurely walks in a peaceful setting. Read more

The Lake Volta

7

The world's largest artificial lake spans 8,502 square kilometers. It serves as a crucial source of electricity for Ghana and its neighboring countries while also thriving with tourism and biodiversity. Explore concealed coves and tranquil inlets as you embark on a kayak or canoe journey, offering a serene escape for nature enthusiasts. Read more

Mole Park

8

Mole National Park in Ghana stands as the country's largest protected area and is renowned for its abundant wildlife, including elephants and antelopes. Its expansive savannahs and guided safaris provide an authentic African ecotourism experience. Read more

Larbadi Beach

9

Labadi Beach in Accra, Ghana, is a well-loved coastal destination renowned for swimming, cultural events, and its vibrant atmosphere. It has gained fame for its local cuisine, music, and breathtaking sunsets. Read more

Okomfo Anokye Sword Site

10

Located on the grounds of the Komfo Anokye Teaching Hospital in Kumasi, the Komfo Anokye Sword Site is a significant historical site. Here, you'll encounter an over 300-year-old sword firmly embedded in the rock face, serving as a central artifact and legend in the rich history of the Asante nation. Many have attempted to extract the sword from the ground, but their efforts have proven unsuccessful due to the Okomfo's declaration when he originally planted it.
